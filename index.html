What is the difference between AC and DC motors?

Posted by User2 in category: Electronics

AC (Alternating Current) and DC (Direct Current) motors are two primary types of electric motors, each with distinct operating principles and characteristics. AC motors rely on alternating current power sources, where the direction of electric current periodically reverses. This reversal typically occurs at a frequency of 50 or 60 times per second, depending on the electrical system's frequency.


On the other hand, DC motors operate on direct current, where the electric current flows in a continuous, unidirectional manner. Their direction of rotation is determined by the polarity of the DC voltage applied to them, necessitating a reversal of the voltage to change the direction of rotation.
